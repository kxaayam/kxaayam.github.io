Engineering Handbook

Personal Reference Guide to Design Thinking Methods and Tools

Frame

The Frame phase involves defining the problem space and establishing project boundaries.

Stakeholder Mapping

Frame

A method for identifying and categorizing all individuals, groups, or organizations that have an interest in or influence over a project.

Used in Praxis I to identify community members, local authorities, and end-users for the water filtration system.

Created a comprehensive map that revealed previously overlooked stakeholders, leading to more inclusive design decisions.

Highly effective for complex projects with multiple stakeholders. Strength: Provides clear visualization. Limitation: Can become overwhelming with too many stakeholders.

Problem Statement Refinement

Frame

The process of transforming vague problem descriptions into clear, actionable statements that guide the design process.

Applied in the EWB Hackathon to clarify irrigation needs from broad "water access" to specific "automated, solar-powered systems for small farms."

Resulted in focused design criteria and measurable success metrics for the project.

Essential for project success. Strength: Improves team alignment. Limitation: Requires multiple iterations to achieve clarity.

Design Criteria Development

Frame

Establishing measurable requirements and constraints that solutions must meet.

Developed criteria for the Praxis II mobility device including weight limits, adjustability ranges, and safety standards.

Criteria guided prototype iterations and ensured the final design met user needs and regulatory requirements.

Critical for technical validation. Strength: Provides objective evaluation framework. Limitation: May limit creative exploration if too restrictive.

Diverge

The Diverge phase focuses on generating multiple solution concepts and exploring possibilities.

Brainstorming Sessions

Diverge

Structured group ideation technique to generate a large quantity of ideas without immediate judgment.

Conducted multiple brainstorming sessions for the water filtration project to explore various filtration mechanisms and material options.

Generated over 50 initial concepts, leading to innovative combinations of local materials and proven filtration techniques.

Excellent for idea generation. Strength: Encourages creativity. Limitation: Requires skilled facilitation to maintain productivity.

Analogous Inspiration

Diverge

Drawing inspiration from solutions in different domains or contexts to apply to the current problem.

Looked at automotive suspension systems for inspiration in designing the mobility device's adjustable mechanisms.

Adapted automotive quick-release mechanisms for the assistive device, improving ease of use and reliability.

Valuable for breakthrough solutions. Strength: Expands solution space. Limitation: Requires broad knowledge to identify relevant analogies.

Converge

The Converge phase involves narrowing down options and selecting the most promising solutions.

Decision Matrix Analysis

Converge

A systematic method for evaluating alternatives against multiple criteria using weighted scoring.

Used to select the optimal irrigation control algorithm from three competing approaches in the EWB project.

The matrix revealed that the hybrid algorithm scored highest across efficiency, reliability, and implementation complexity criteria.

Effective for objective decision-making. Strength: Transparent and repeatable. Limitation: Requires clear criteria definition upfront.

Prototype Testing Cycles

Converge

Iterative building and testing of scaled-down versions of solutions to gather feedback and refine concepts.

Created five prototype iterations for the mobility device, testing ergonomics, weight distribution, and user comfort at each stage.

Each iteration improved user satisfaction scores, with the final prototype achieving 85% positive feedback in usability testing.

Essential for user-centered design. Strength: Provides empirical feedback. Limitation: Time and resource intensive.

Represent

The Represent phase focuses on communicating design solutions and findings to stakeholders.

Technical Documentation

Represent

Creating comprehensive written and visual documentation of design processes, decisions, and specifications.

Developed detailed technical documentation for the water filtration system including assembly instructions, maintenance procedures, and performance specifications.

Documentation enabled local communities to manufacture and maintain the systems independently, extending project impact beyond the design phase.

Critical for project sustainability. Strength: Enables knowledge transfer. Limitation: Time-consuming to create comprehensive documentation.

Visual Presentation Design

Represent

Creating compelling visual representations of design solutions for different audiences and purposes.

Designed presentation materials for the Praxis II project including 3D renderings, user journey maps, and impact visualizations for stakeholder presentations.

Visual materials effectively communicated complex technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders, securing additional funding for prototype development.

Essential for stakeholder engagement. Strength: Improves comprehension. Limitation: Requires design skills beyond technical expertise.
